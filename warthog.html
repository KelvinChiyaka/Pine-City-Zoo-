WARTHOG

The warthog (Phacochoerus africanus) is a wild member of the pig family, native to sub-Saharan Africa. They are easily recognized by their distinctive tusks and facial warts. Warthogs are primarily herbivorous, feeding on grasses, roots, and fruits.

Warthogs are known for their unique behavior of kneeling on their front knees while feeding, which allows them to reach low vegetation. They are social animals, often found in small groups called sounders, typically consisting of females and their young, while males tend to be more solitary or form bachelor groups. Warthogs play an important role in their ecosystem by helping to aerate the soil and control vegetation growth through their foraging activities.
