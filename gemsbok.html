GEMSBOK

The gemsbok (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope species native to the southern African region. It is known for its striking appearance, with long, straight horns and a distinctive black and white facial pattern. Gemsboks are well adapted to arid environments, primarily inhabiting savannas and semi-desert areas. They are social animals, often found in herds, and are known for their endurance and ability to survive in harsh conditions.

Gemsboks are herbivores, primarily grazing on grasses and browsing on shrubs. They have a unique ability to regulate their body temperature, allowing them to thrive in extreme heat. Their horns, which can reach lengths of up to 1 meter (3.3 feet), are used for defense against predators such as lions and hyenas.
