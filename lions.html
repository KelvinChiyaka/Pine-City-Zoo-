LION

The lion (Panthera leo) is a large carnivorous feline native to Africa and parts of Asia. Known as the "king of the jungle," lions are social animals that live in groups called prides, which typically consist of related females and their offspring, along with a small number of adult males. Lions are apex predators and play a crucial role in their ecosystem.

Lions are known for their majestic manes, which vary in color and size among individuals. They are powerful hunters, primarily preying on large ungulates such as zebras and wildebeests. Despite their strength, lions face threats from habitat loss and human-wildlife conflict, leading to a decline in their populations in the wild.
