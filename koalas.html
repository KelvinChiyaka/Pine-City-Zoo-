KOALA

Koalas are marsupials native to Australia, known for their distinctive appearance with large ears and a stout, tailless body. They primarily inhabit eucalyptus forests and are famous for their diet of eucalyptus leaves, which are low in nutrition and require them to sleep up to 20 hours a day to conserve energy.

Koalas are solitary animals, typically living alone except for mothers with their young. They have a unique vocalization that can be heard during the breeding season, which sounds like a deep bellow. Koalas play a crucial role in their ecosystem by helping to maintain the health of eucalyptus forests through their feeding habits.
