PANDA

The giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) is a bear species native to China, known for its distinctive black and white fur. Pandas primarily inhabit bamboo forests in the mountainous regions of central China, where they spend most of their time eating bamboo, which makes up about 99% of their diet. Despite being classified as carnivores, they have adapted to a herbivorous diet.

Pandas are solitary animals, except during mating season or when a mother is raising her cubs. They are known for their gentle nature and playful behavior, especially when young. Pandas play a crucial role in their ecosystem by helping to maintain the health of bamboo forests through their feeding habits. Conservation efforts have been implemented to protect their natural habitat and increase their population, as they were once considered endangered.
