GIRAFFE

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, known for their long necks and legs. They inhabit savannas and open woodlands in Africa, primarily feeding on leaves from tall trees. Their unique coat patterns help them blend into their environment, providing camouflage from predators.

Giraffes are social animals, often found in loose herds. They have a distinctive gait, moving both legs on one side of their body at the same time, which gives them a unique walking style. Giraffes communicate through various vocalizations and body language, including necking, a behavior where males swing their necks to establish dominance or compete for mates.
